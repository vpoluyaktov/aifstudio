    no Standard Rules action called "reading".

    phrasing will be misdetected as a failure. Do not use plain 'say' for

    must have a matching '[end if]'. Forgetting '[end if]' causes the
    rest of the description to be treated as conditional text and often
    triggers a compile error.
    Wrong:   say "[if the lamp is lit]It is bright.[otherwise]It is dark."
    Correct: say "[if the lamp is lit]It is bright.[otherwise]It is dark.[end if]"

    for supporters.
    Wrong:   "On the box is a key." when the box is a container.
    Correct: "In the box is a key."

    rules and descriptions.

    Wrong:   "[one of]A bird sings[or]A dog barks."
    Correct: "[one of]A bird sings[or]A dog barks[at random]."
29. Text substitutions in square brackets ([if ...], [one of],
    [the noun], etc.) are only valid inside a quoted "..." string.
    Never write them as free-standing source lines.
    Wrong:   [if the lantern is lit] say "It glows." [end if]
    Correct: say "[if the lantern is lit]It glows.[otherwise]It is dark.[end if]";

    Correct: The oak door is lockable and locked. The brass key unlocks the oak door.

    Wrong:   Carry out switching on the lantern: the lantern is lit.
    Correct: Carry out switching on the lantern: now the lantern is lit;
